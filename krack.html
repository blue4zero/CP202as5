Are you vulnerable?

What is Krack.

A flaw found within the Wi-Fi's WPA security protocol leaves you vulnerable for hackers to view your data while you are connected to Wi-Fi. This security breach known as KRACK, affects the Wi-Fi protocol itself, so itâ€™s not something simply fixed with a reboot or changing products. Luckily, major tech companies are working to patch the breach. KRACK is a method that targets the third step in the four-step authentication of the protocol. During the third step, an encryption key will be sent several times for authentication, attackers will collect those transmission in a specific way and allow them to break the Wi-Fi security encryption.

How you can stay protected.

The biggest thing you can do is to keep your devices up-to-date. If your device does not have an update available yet, use a wired Ethernet connection if possible. While using public hotspots, only use secured websites that uses HTTPS encryption (unsecured will use HTTP).
